OCR — Optical Character Recognition

Extracts the visible, human-readable fields: name, date of birth, document number, nationality, expiry date.

MRZ — Machine Readable Zone

Parses the machine-encoded strip at the bottom of passports and IDs — a standardized, tamper-resistant format encoding the same identity data.

Cross-check

OCR and MRZ data are compared automatically. If they don't match, the verification is flagged — catching tampered documents a single-layer read would miss.

How much does identity verification cost?

Identity verification is an add-on feature available on paid plans. Pricing is per verification — you only pay when a guest completes the process. It's designed to pay for itself by preventing just one fraudulent booking.

Is the biometric data secure and GDPR compliant?

Yes. All biometric data is encrypted in transit and at rest. Chekin is fully GDPR-compliant, ISO certified, and ENISA-compliant. Data is retained only for the legally required period and then automatically deleted.

What happens if a verification fails?

If the biometric comparison doesn't produce a confident match, the guest is prompted to retry. If it still fails, you're notified and can choose to verify manually from your Chekin dashboard.

Which identity documents are supported?

Chekin supports passports, national identity cards, driving licences, and residence permits from over 150 countries. The OCR engine is trained on more than 2,500 document templates.

What is the difference between selfie and liveness verification?

Selfie match compares a single photo against the document photo — it's the fastest method. Liveness detection adds a short video sequence with on-screen prompts, confirming a real person is present and blocking spoofing attempts.
